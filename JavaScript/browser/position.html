The following questions arose in my mind, to answer which, I made this example. They are:

  • What does the parentElement.nodeName.toLowerCase() (or parent().prop("nodeName").toLowerCase() in jQuery) property of a div read if it is absolutely positioned?
  • And what if it has a separate layer of its own, i.e. it has a non-zero z-index?
  • And in such a case, is its containing block the same as its parent?

We try that out with the divs below.

The first div with the id divInTheBodyElement is inside the body element. There are two divs that are inside a div with the id outer. One of them, named the firstChild has no separate z-index or layer of its own; the other child, with the id secondChild, has a z-index of 1.

The divs with the ids divInTheBodyElement, outer, firstChild and secondChild have their position property set to absolute.

Another div with the id otherOuter has the default position, i.e. static. It has two child divs, the first of which has the id child1 has no separate layer of its own. The second, child2 has the z-index value of 2.

From trying the example, here are the conclusions:

  • What does the parent() property of a div read if it is absolutely positioned?

    The parent always refers to the parent element in the DOM, regardless of the position or the z-index properties.

  • And what if it has a separate layer of its own, i.e. it has a non-zero z-index?

    The answer to the previous bullet point answers this as well.

  • And in such a case, is its containing block the same as its parent?

    No, the containing block is not the same as the parent. As can be seen in the example above, #child1 and #child2, the two divs inside the #otherOuter div, have their parent as the #otherOuter, but they are positioned and sized as per the dimensions of the viewport, which becomes their containing block. Try sizing the window to different sizes and see their position and sizes change while the position and sizes of the other divs remain constant as they are relative to their containing blocks, which are their parents, because their immediate parents are also positioned absolutely.
